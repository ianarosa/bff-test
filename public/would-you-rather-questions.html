120 Would You Rather Questions for Friends

Ask someone an open question and they'll hand you something flattering and shapeless. "What matters to you?" gets you family, honesty, being present — true, warm, and worth nothing, because nobody has ever answered that question with money and being left alone. A forced choice closes that escape route. Give a person two things and make them drop one, and the drop is the information. You don't learn what someone values from a paragraph of self-description; you learn it from watching what they're willing to lose. Which is why the good would-you-rathers are never balanced between one appealing option and one obviously terrible one. The good ones hurt both ways, or tempt both ways, and the person has to stand somewhere. Here are 120 of them, written in your voice — the one being guessed about.

1. Everyday dilemmas

These are the small ones, and people underrate them badly. Nobody's identity is on the line when they choose between a bad commute and a bad chair, so the answer comes out honest and unguarded — which is exactly what makes it revealing. A friend who can predict how you'd trade off sleep against a decent morning knows the texture of your actual life, not the version of it you'd describe at a party. Start a quiz here to warm people up before you ask them anything that stings.

  1. Would I rather lose an hour of sleep or skip breakfast entirely?
  2. Would I rather have a two-hour commute with a seat or a twenty-minute one standing pressed against a stranger?
  3. Would I rather do all my laundry or all my dishes for the rest of my life?
  4. Would I rather have no hot water for a week or no wifi for a week?
  5. Would I rather always be slightly too hot or slightly too cold?
  6. Would I rather live somewhere with a brilliant kitchen and a terrible bathroom, or the reverse?
  7. Would I rather take a phone call or reply to eleven texts?
  8. Would I rather have a five-minute walk to work and hate the job, or love the job and commute an hour?
  9. Would I rather every shoe I own be uncomfortable or every chair I sit in be slightly wrong?
  10. Would I rather assemble furniture with no instructions or with instructions I'm not allowed to skim?
  11. Would I rather never find a parking space on the first pass or never find my keys on the first look?
  12. Would I rather be interrupted constantly or left completely alone all day?
  13. Would I rather queue for forty minutes for something excellent or get something fine immediately?
  14. Would I rather have a perfect mattress and a broken pillow or a perfect pillow and a broken mattress?
  15. Would I rather do a big terrifying task first thing or let it sit on me until 4pm?
  16. Would I rather always have a full battery and no charger, or a charger and a battery that dies at 30%?
  17. Would I rather live somewhere with no shops and total quiet, or everything nearby and constant noise?
  18. Would I rather clean the whole flat in one brutal Saturday or ten minutes every single day?

2. Food crimes

Nothing starts a real argument faster than food, and that's the point — these are the questions where the table gets loud. People hold food opinions with an intensity completely out of proportion to the stakes, which means friends actually remember your answers. Pick the ones you've already ranted about out loud; if you've made someone sit through your position on it, it's a good question.

  1. Would I rather never eat cheese again or never drink coffee again?
  2. Would I rather give up bread or give up chocolate?
  3. Would I rather eat only sweet food forever or only savoury?
  4. Would I rather have an incredible meal I can never repeat or a very good one I can eat weekly?
  5. Would I rather lose my sense of smell or eat every meal lukewarm?
  6. Would I rather cook every meal myself or never be allowed in a kitchen again?
  7. Would I rather eat pineapple on pizza every week or never eat pizza again?
  8. Would I rather have unlimited takeaway but no dessert, or unlimited dessert and no takeaway?
  9. Would I rather eat the same breakfast every day or a random surprise breakfast I can't refuse?
  10. Would I rather food be free but bland, or expensive and extraordinary?
  11. Would I rather give up fizzy drinks or give up crisps?
  12. Would I rather eat a food I hate once a month or a food I love every day until I hate it?
  13. Would I rather always overcook things slightly or always undersalt them?
  14. Would I rather eat alone in a great restaurant or with friends somewhere mediocre?
  15. Would I rather have terrible coffee at home or a brilliant coffee shop with a twenty-minute queue?
  16. Would I rather never have a second helping or never have a first bite of anything new?
  17. Would I rather be a chef who can't taste or a critic who can't cook?

3. Money and work

Everyone claims money isn't the point right up until you make them choose. These questions are useful precisely because the socially correct answer is obvious and the honest one usually isn't — so watch which way you actually lean before you write your options. A friend who can call your answer here knows something about you that most colleagues never will.

  1. Would I rather earn double and work weekends, or earn half and never think about work after 5pm?
  2. Would I rather have a boss who's incompetent and kind or brilliant and cruel?
  3. Would I rather be paid in a lump sum once a year or a steady trickle every week?
  4. Would I rather work alone forever or never work alone again?
  5. Would I rather be great at a job nobody respects or mediocre at one everyone admires?
  6. Would I rather have four days off and one brutal workday, or five easy ones?
  7. Would I rather retire at 40 with barely enough or at 65 with plenty?
  8. Would I rather my salary be public to everyone I know or never know anyone else's?
  9. Would I rather have job security and no growth or constant growth and no security?
  10. Would I rather win a modest amount of money now or a fortune in twenty years?
  11. Would I rather be famous in my field or rich outside it?
  12. Would I rather manage people or never be responsible for anyone again?
  13. Would I rather do work I'm proud of that nobody sees, or work I'm embarrassed by that everybody does?
  14. Would I rather have unlimited holiday I feel guilty taking or twenty days I take without a thought?
  15. Would I rather lose my savings or lose ten years of my career progress?
  16. Would I rather have a job that ends at a clear time or one that never really ends but pays for everything?
  17. Would I rather be the person who started it or the person who fixed it?

4. Friendship and loyalty

This is where people find out what they think they owe each other, and the answers are rarely as generous as anyone expects. A friendship question is only good if choosing costs something — if there's a version where you look like a bad friend and you'd pick it anyway. Use two or three of these in a quiz and put them in the middle, not the end.

  1. Would I rather be told a hard truth immediately or a kind lie that holds for a year?
  2. Would I rather have one friend who'd drop everything or six who'd all send a nice text?
  3. Would I rather forgive someone who never apologised or get an apology from someone I'm done with?
  4. Would I rather my friends be honest about my worst quality or never mention it?
  5. Would I rather be the one everyone calls in a crisis or the one nobody worries about?
  6. Would I rather lend a friend money and never get it back, or refuse and keep the money?
  7. Would I rather know a secret I can't tell anyone or be the one everybody's talking about?
  8. Would I rather miss a friend's big day for a very good reason or attend and resent it?
  9. Would I rather keep a friendship that's gone stale or end it cleanly and lose the history?
  10. Would I rather be liked by everyone slightly or loved by three people completely?
  11. Would I rather find out a friend lied to protect me or told the truth and hurt me?
  12. Would I rather always be the one who reaches out first or never be reached out to?
  13. Would I rather my best friend move abroad or take a job that eats all their time here?
  14. Would I rather be forgiven quickly or actually understood slowly?
  15. Would I rather have my friends read every message I've sent about them, or read every message they've sent about me?
  16. Would I rather help a friend with something enormous once or something small every week for a decade?
  17. Would I rather be someone's favourite person or someone's most trusted one?

5. Pure chaos

No correct answer, no moral, no lesson — these exist purely to make the group chat lose its mind. They also do quiet structural work in a quiz: they break the tension between the heavy sections and give people something to laugh about before you ask them something sharp. The comedy comes from the fact that you genuinely do have an answer, and your real friends genuinely know it.

  1. Would I rather fight one horse-sized duck or a hundred duck-sized horses?
  2. Would I rather have hands for feet or feet for hands?
  3. Would I rather sneeze glitter or sweat maple syrup?
  4. Would I rather have a permanent laugh track or dramatic music that swells at bad moments?
  5. Would I rather everything I say rhyme or everything I write be in all caps?
  6. Would I rather be able to teleport only to places I've been, or fly at walking speed?
  7. Would I rather have a tail I can't control or ears that move when I lie?
  8. Would I rather always know when someone's lying or always be believed when I do?
  9. Would I rather be trailed by a documentary crew forever or narrated by a stranger nobody else hears?
  10. Would I rather have every meal appear as a smoothie or as a pill?
  11. Would I rather be permanently three inches taller or permanently five minutes early?
  12. Would I rather speak to animals but they're all rude, or read minds but only about the weather?
  13. Would I rather my phone play my search history aloud or my camera roll show on any screen I stand near?
  14. Would I rather live in a world with no music or no fiction?
  15. Would I rather have one arm that's enormously strong or two that never get tired?
  16. Would I rather age backwards from now or stay exactly this age forever?
  17. Would I rather every stranger think we're old friends or every old friend think I'm a stranger?

6. Deep cuts

These look calm and aren't. Each one is a trade between two things a person actually wants — being known and being safe, being right and being liked — and there's no version where you get both. The reason they're so satisfying to get right is that nobody has ever announced their answer to these at dinner. If a friend calls it correctly, they worked it out from watching you, which is the whole thing this quiz is trying to measure.

  1. Would I rather be completely understood by one person or admired by everyone?
  2. Would I rather know how people really see me or never have to find out?
  3. Would I rather be remembered for one thing or remembered warmly for nothing in particular?
  4. Would I rather fail at something I chose or succeed at something I fell into?
  5. Would I rather be the smartest person in the room or the one everyone's glad came?
  6. Would I rather have my regrets erased or my best memories sharpened?
  7. Would I rather be right in an argument I lose or wrong in one I win?
  8. Would I rather know exactly how my life turns out or genuinely never wonder?
  9. Would I rather be needed or be free?
  10. Would I rather my worst moment be public and forgiven, or private and never resolved?
  11. Would I rather have more time or more energy?
  12. Would I rather be brave once when it counted or reliably kind for forty years?
  13. Would I rather feel everything more intensely or a little less than I do now?
  14. Would I rather be told I'm doing enough or told exactly what to do?
  15. Would I rather people know how hard something was for me or think it came easily?
  16. Would I rather lose my ambition or lose my contentment?
  17. Would I rather live a quiet life I chose or an extraordinary one I didn't?

7. The brutal ones

Save these for last. Both options genuinely hurt, there's no clever way out, and that's what makes them the best closers a quiz can have — the answer is never a joke and the guess is never lazy. There's real risk here, so use them with people you trust to take the answer kindly. One or two at the end of a quiz will be the thing everyone's still arguing about a week later.

  1. Would I rather lose every photo I've ever taken or every message I've ever sent?
  2. Would I rather forget a year of my life or have a year of it played publicly?
  3. Would I rather know the date I die or the way?
  4. Would I rather lose my hearing or my sight?
  5. Would I rather never see my oldest friend again or see them every week and have it be awkward forever?
  6. Would I rather relive my worst day once a year or never have my best one back?
  7. Would I rather be the one who leaves or the one who's left?
  8. Would I rather lose ten years at the end of my life or ten years of memory from the middle?
  9. Would I rather everyone I love outlive me or the reverse?
  10. Would I rather be forgotten by everyone I've helped or remembered only for my worst decision?
  11. Would I rather have said the thing and been rejected, or never said it and never known?
  12. Would I rather spend my life in a city I love with nobody I know, or a city I can't stand with everyone I love?
  13. Would I rather my children be happy and ordinary or brilliant and troubled?
  14. Would I rather be someone's biggest regret or their biggest what-if?
  15. Would I rather know a truth that ruins a good memory or keep the memory and be a fool?
  16. Would I rather be forgiven for something I did or blamed for something I didn't?
  17. Would I rather have twenty more years and know it, or a life of unknown length that ends whenever?

How to use these in a quiz

The rule that matters most: pick the ones where your answer would surprise people. If your friends could guess it from knowing you for three weeks, it's a wasted slot. The gold is in the questions where you'd pick the option nobody expects — the one where you'd take the money, or leave first, or admit you'd rather be admired than understood. Those are the questions that make a leaderboard mean something.

And cut anything where one option is obviously correct. "Would I rather be rich or be in constant pain" isn't a question, it's a formality — everyone gets it right and nobody learns a thing. The test is simple: if you can imagine two reasonable friends genuinely disagreeing about which you'd pick, keep it. If the argument would be over in four seconds, bin it. The same logic applies to any question you write, and it's covered in more depth in the 100 questions list, where the wrong answers matter as much as the right one.

Would-you-rathers have one enormous practical advantage: they translate perfectly into this app. You set the answer options yourself, so a forced choice becomes a clean two-option question with no filler — no inventing two plausible-but-fake decoys, no padding. Two real options, both live, both defensible, one of them true. Mix a few of these in among your specific-answer questions and the quiz gets a rhythm to it: something they can know, then something they have to read you to get.
